Xviii.

ENDIVES AND LETTUCE INTUBA ET LACTUCÆ

ENDIVES [are dressed] WITH BRINE, A LITTLE OIL AND CHOPPED ONION, INSTEAD OF THE REAL LETTUCE [1] IN WINTER TIME THE ENDIVES ARE TAKEN OUT OF THE PICKLE [2] [and are dressed] WITH HONEY OR VINEGAR.

[1] Hum. _pro lactucis uere_; Tor. _p. l. accipint_;
G.-V. _p. l. vero_ (separated by period)—all indicating
that endives are a substitute for lettuce when this is
not
